from a Distance

Remote sensing is our cosmic toolkit for studying planets without ever leaving orbit. We use light, radio waves, and more to unveil the secrets of distant worlds.

Passive Sensing

Detects natural energy, like reflected sunlight or heat emitted by a planet, to see what it's made of.

Active Sensing

Sends out its own signal (like radar or laser) and analyzes the echo that bounces back to map surfaces.

The Electromagnetic Spectrum

Ultraviolet (UV)

Finds auroras & atmospheric gases.

Visible (VIS)

Shows surface color and features.

Near-Infrared (NIR)

Identifies minerals and water ice.

Thermal IR (TIR)

Measures surface temperature.

Radar/Microwave

Maps topography and peers under ice.

Mission Highlights

Mars: Found evidence of ancient water in clay minerals using spectrometers.
Venus: Mapped the entire surface through thick clouds with the Magellan radar.
The Moon: Created ultra-high-resolution 3D maps using laser altimetry (LOLA).
Asteroids: Mapped the shape and composition of Bennu & Ryugu for sample return.
Europa & Ganymede: Planning to probe icy crusts with radar on the JUICE mission.
Titan: Pierced the hazy atmosphere with radar to reveal lakes of liquid methane.

Current Challenges

  • Atmospheric dust and gas blocking the view
  • Limited power and data transmission from deep space
  • Low light conditions in the outer solar system
  • Calibrating instruments without ground truth data

The Future is Bright

  • Miniature sensors on tiny CubeSats
  • AI-powered analysis to find features automatically
  • Fusing data from multiple instruments into 3D models
  • Smarter spacecraft that process data on board
