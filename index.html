I specialize in observational astronomy and planetary systems analysis, with expertise in computational methods for space science research. My research focuses on airglow and auroral emissions, atmospheric science, and data analysis of space-based observations.

I am committed to open science principles, reproducible research workflows, and developing long-lived scientific software. My work emphasizes rigorous computational methods, transparent data analysis, and making research accessible to the broader scientific community.

Currently transitioning from MATLAB to Python for scientific computing, developing atmospheric science models, analyzing space mission data (NASA, ESA, ISRO, JAXA), and creating educational resources for students and researchers interested in planetary science.

Active science communicator through blogging on WordPress and Medium, sharing insights on planetary science, space missions, and computational methods. Committed to making complex scientific concepts accessible to diverse audiences.

Research Expertise

Atmospheric Emissions

Investigation of upper atmospheric light emissions, their spectral characteristics, and physical processes. Analysis of observational data from multiple space missions and ground-based observations.

Planetary Atmospheres

Modeling and characterization of planetary atmospheric phenomena. Study of chemical composition, thermal structure, and dynamics. Application to exoplanet science and comparative planetology.

Space Mission Data

Processing and analysis of data from NASA, ESA, ISRO, and JAXA missions. Development of algorithms for data reduction, quality assurance, and scientific interpretation.

Computational Methods

High-performance numerical methods, scientific visualization, machine learning applications in astronomy, and development of reproducible computational workflows.

Open Science

Data Science

Advanced techniques in data science, interactive visualizations, and statistical analysis for understanding complex astronomical phenomena and research patterns.
