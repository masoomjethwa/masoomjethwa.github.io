A JOURNEY THROUGH THE COSMOS

Our universe is vast, ancient, and filled with mysteries. This guide visualizes our best understanding of where it all came from, what it's made of, and where it might be going, based on the evidence we've gathered so far.

What is the Universe Made Of?

The most startling discovery in modern cosmology is that everything we can see—stars, planets, galaxies—makes up less than 5% of the universe. The rest is composed of mysterious dark matter and even more enigmatic dark energy.

The Standard Model: Evidence for the Big Bang

The Big Bang is not just a theory, it's a model overwhelmingly supported by three independent pillars of observational evidence. Together, they paint a consistent picture of a universe that started hot and dense ~13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding and cooling ever since.

1

Cosmological Redshift

In 1929, Edwin Hubble observed that distant galaxies are moving away from us, and the farther they are, the faster they recede. This stretching of light waves, known as redshift, is direct evidence that the fabric of space itself is expanding.

Cosmic Microwave Background

The CMB is the "afterglow" of the Big Bang—a faint, uniform radiation with a temperature of 2.725 Kelvin permeating all of space. Discovered in 1964, it's a direct snapshot of the universe when it was just 380,000 years old.

Light Element Abundances

In the first few minutes, the universe was a nuclear furnace. The Big Bang model precisely predicts the primordial ratio of elements created: ~75% Hydrogen and ~25% Helium, which is exactly what we observe in the oldest stars and gas clouds.

Showdown: Big Bang vs. Steady State

The Mystery of Dark Matter

Galaxies spin much faster than they should based on their visible matter. This discrepancy is a key piece of evidence for dark matter—an invisible "halo" of mass providing the extra gravitational glue needed to hold galaxies together.

The Enigma of Dark Energy

In 1998, astronomers discovered the expansion of the universe is not slowing down—it's accelerating. This is attributed to dark energy, a mysterious repulsive force that acts like anti-gravity on cosmic scales.

Beyond the Standard Model: Frontier Theories

Science never stops asking questions. While the Big Bang model is robust, physicists are exploring many mind-bending alternative theories to push the boundaries of our knowledge. Here are a few.

Ekpyrotic/Cyclic Universe

Suggests our universe is one of many in an endless cycle of collision, expansion, and contraction, born from the clash of higher-dimensional "branes."

Holographic Universe

Posits that our 3D reality is a projection of information encoded on a distant 2D surface, much like a hologram.

Multiverse Theory

Proposes our universe is just one of a vast, possibly infinite, collection of other universes, each with potentially different laws of physics.
