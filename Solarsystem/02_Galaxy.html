Our Galactic Neighborhood

The Milky Way isn't alone. It's a leading member of the Local Group, a gravitationally bound collection of over 54 galaxies. Our cosmic family is a dynamic system, with galaxies constantly interacting and heading for future mergers.

Major Local Group Galaxies by Size

Comparing the estimated star count of the three largest galaxies in our Local Group. The Andromeda galaxy is our largest neighbor and is on a collision course with the Milky Way, set to merge in about 4.5 billion years.

Echoes from the Dawn of Time

Looking deep into space is looking back in time. There we can see quasars, the most luminous objects in the universe, which were far more common when the cosmos was young. They are powered by supermassive black holes feeding ferociously.
