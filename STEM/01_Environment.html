üü¢ GRADE 3: Exploring Nature Through Senses

Theme: Observing the Environment

1. Mini Water Cycle in a Bag

Description: Ziplock bag + water + marker. Tape on a window to observe condensation.

Outcomes: Understand basic water cycle (evaporation, condensation, precipitation).

Duration: 15 mins setup, observe over days.

Skills Developed: Observation, Water Cycle understanding.

Materials:
  • Ziplock bag (quart or gallon size)
  • Water
  • Blue food coloring (optional, for visibility)
  • Permanent marker
  • Tape (strong adhesive like masking or packing tape)

2. Seed Sprouter Model

Description: Use cotton and clear cups to sprout seeds.

Outcomes: Learn plant life cycle stages (germination, root/shoot growth).

Duration: 15 mins setup, observe over 1-2 weeks.

Skills Developed: Plant life cycle, patience, observation.

Materials:
  • Clear plastic cups or jars
  • Cotton balls or paper towels
  • Various seeds (e.g., beans, lentils, radish seeds)
  • Water

3. DIY Wind Vane

Description: Make a working wind direction tool using straws and a bottle cap.

Outcomes: Understand wind direction and basic weather tools.

Duration: 30-45 mins.

Skills Developed: Air movement, basic mechanics.

Materials:
  • 2 straws
  • Pencil with eraser
  • Pushpin
  • Paper plate
  • Plastic bottle cap (from a water bottle)
  • Clay or play-doh (for base)
  • Markers (for N, S, E, W)
  • Scissors

4. Pollination Bee Game

Description: Cotton ball ‚Äúbees‚Äù carry chalk ‚Äúpollen‚Äù between paper flowers.

Outcomes: Grasp pollination's role in plant reproduction.

Duration: 20-30 mins.

Skills Developed: Pollination concept, fine motor skills.

Materials:
  • Construction paper (various colors for flowers)
  • Cotton balls ("bees")
  • Chalk or powdered tempera paint (for "pollen")
  • Scissors
  • Glue or tape
  • Optional: String/yarn for "bee" path

5. Mini Compost Jar

Description: Layer organic and inorganic materials in a clear jar. Watch decomposition.

Outcomes: Understand decomposition and waste management.

Duration: 20 mins setup, observe over weeks.

Skills Developed: Waste management, observation, understanding cycles.

Materials:
  • Clear glass jar with lid (e.g., mason jar)
  • Soil
  • Small pieces of fruit/vegetable scraps (e.g., apple core, banana peel)
  • Dry leaves or small twigs
  • Small plastic toy or piece of foil (non-biodegradable example)
  • Water (to moisten)

6. Rain Catcher Model

Description: Make a simple rain gauge from a bottle. Track rainfall.

Outcomes: Measure precipitation and understand weather data collection.

Duration: 20-30 mins setup, ongoing observation.

Skills Developed: Data collection, measurement, weather observation.

Materials:
  • Large clear plastic bottle (e.g., 2-liter soda bottle)
  • Ruler
  • Scissors
  • Permanent marker
  • Tape
  • Small rocks or pebbles (for stability)
