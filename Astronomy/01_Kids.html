Origin of the Universe

Scientists have two main ideas for how it all started. The Big Bang is the most accepted one!

Big Bang Theory

Everything started from a tiny, hot dot and has been expanding like a balloon for 13.8 billion years!

Steady State Theory

An older idea that the universe always existed and new stuff is always being made as it expands.

Our Galaxy: The Milky Way

The Sun's Recipe

The Sun is a giant ball of hot gas. It's mostly made of Hydrogen and Helium, which it burns to create light and heat.

Morning & Evening Star

That bright "star" you see right after sunset or before sunrise is usually not a star at all! It's the planet Venus, shining brightly because its thick clouds reflect sunlight so well.

Is Earth's Spin Slowing Down?

Yes! The Moon's gravity pulls on our oceans, creating tides. This friction acts like a tiny brake, making our days very, very slowly longer over millions of years.

Globe, Time, & Space Tech

Understanding space helps us right here on Earth!

  • Latitude & Longitude: Imaginary lines on the globe that help us create maps and find any location.
  • Time Zones: Because the Earth spins, we use longitude to create 24 time zones around the world.
  • Applied Astronomy: GPS in our phones, weather satellites, and even medical scanners are technologies that came from space research!
